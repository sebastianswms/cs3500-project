Location

The Amazon River is located in South America, and stretches across Peru, Columbia, and Brazil. The source of the Amazon River is the Ucayali-Apurímac river system, and it flows for over 6400 km before finally emptying into the Atlantic Ocean [2].

Statistics

  • Average Discharge: 300,000 m3/s [3]
  • Length: 6400 km [2] (disputed)
  • Basin Size: 7,000,000 km2 [2]

Other Information

Discovery

In 1541, Francisco de Orellana became the first European to discover the Amazon River, although it had been known to the indigenous locals for many years. It is believed that the name "Amazon" was a reference by Orellana to the mythical Amazonians of Asia. [3]

Environmental Significance

There are over 1000 known tributaries that flow into the Amazon, making it a part of one of the most involved river systems in the world [3]. Furthermore, the massive amounts of freshwater that flow through the Amazon dilute the Atlantic Ocean even up to 160 km from its mouth [2].

Peru's Modern-Day Plans

Because the Amazon River's system extends over approximately 60% of Peru's national territory, the Peruvian government has extensive plans to make use of the Amazon. They intend to build 70 dams on the Amazon River, in addition to constructing 15 hydroelectric power plants as part of an energy deal with Brazil [4].
