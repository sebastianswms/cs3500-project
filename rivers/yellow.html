Location

The Yellow River, or HuangHe, stretches all the way from Western China to the Bahai sea. It has a length of 5,465 km or 3,395 miles [1].

Statistics

  • It has an average flow rate of 1,800 cubic meters per second [4]
  • It has a drainage basin of around 750,000 square kilometers, or 290,000 square miles [2].
  • It is blanketed by approximately 1000 feet of fine, wind-blown soil that gives it its yellow color [3].

Other Information

History

The Yellow River basin is the foundation of Chinese society, and many ancient cities were built around it. It has been the subject of thousands of years of civil engineering to irrigate its water, including hydroelectric dams [2]. It is called Yellow River because it carries yellow-brown silt. As a fun fact, the ancient Mongolian name for the river is “Black River,” because the river is free of silt in the Loess Plateau in Inner Mongolia.

In Modern Day

There are sixteen hydroelectric dams currently installed along the Yellow River. The largest sever of them have a total capacity of 5,618 MW [5].
